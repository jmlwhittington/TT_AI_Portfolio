Suno Music(?) Generations with My Brother

Toward the end of the final semester of my master's program and before a short summer break involving a move across the country to start a PhD program, my brother and I played (and I mean that quite literally) around with a large investment of credits in Suno he made.

I must warn you: some of these are "unsettling." We ended up leaning into the more unusual tags and experimenting with how Suno read "lyrics" as scripts. After a lot of silliness in trying to break it, I compared our later direction to David Lynch film soundtracks or works by Igor Stravinsky.

Unfortunately, some of the prompting information has been lost, but the creative spirit behind it remains and will likely be embarked upon again someday.
